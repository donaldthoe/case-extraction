Kesatuan Eksekutif RHB Bank Berhad v RHB Bank Berhad

No. Kes 4/2-2565/18
Award Award No 233 of 2023
Presiding YA Tuan Augustine Anthony
Pages 12-51
Collective Agreement

Summary

Background/Parties: This case (Award No. 233 of 2023, Case No. 4/4-2565/18) is a trade dispute between Kesatuan Eksekutif RHB Bank Berhad (the Union) and RHB Bank Berhad (the Bank), referred by the Minister of Human Resources under Section 26(2) of the Industrial Relations Act 1967. The dispute concerned the terms of the 1st Collective Agreement for the period 01.01.2014 to 31.12.2016, covering executive employees in Grades R1 to R4. Of 42 proposed articles, 24 were agreed upon, leaving 17 articles in dispute. Key Issues: The disputed articles included Rates of Pay, Annual Increment, Allowances, Hours of Work, Annual Leave, Medical Benefits, Special Leave (Haj), Retrenchment, and others. Court's Finding: The Court resolved each disputed article through unanimous or majority decisions, adopting a mix of Union and Bank proposals based on equity, financial implications, and industry practice. Compensation/Award Details: As a collective agreement dispute, no individual compensation was awarded. The Court ordered specific financial terms incorporated into the 1st CA: annual increments of minimum RM215 (Grades R1-R2) and RM310 (Grades R3-R4); meal allowance of RM20/RM30/RM50 for work outside normal hours (1/2/3+ hours); outstation allowance of RM100 (with accommodation) or RM150 (without); transport reimbursement at RM1.00 per kilometre; shift allowance of RM200 (two-shift) and RM250 (three-shift) per month; NACCS allowance at RM55/RM40 per day; acting allowance of RM100 per day; Forex Booth allowance of RM200 per month; medical benefits with caps of RM4,000 (R1-R3) or RM7,000 (R4) for combined outpatient and hospitalisation, plus Health Flex benefits extended to all employees; annual leave of 22/27/30 working days; and Haj leave of 30 working days.

Case 2

Persatuan Bank-Bank Perdagangan Tanah Melayu v National Union of Bank Employees, States of Malaya

No. Kes 42/478/24
Award Award No 1679 of 2024
Presiding YA Tuan Paramalingam J. Doraisamy
Pages 52-58
Trade Dispute

Summary

Background/Parties: This case (Interim Award No. 1679 of 2024) involves Persatuan Bank-Bank Perdagangan Tanah Melayu (MCBA) and National Union of Bank Employees (the Union). The Union applied for issuance of a subpoena against the Minister of Human Resources, Y.B. Steven Sim Chee Keong, to compel his testimony in a trade dispute concerning the validity of a Memorandum of Agreement on Festival Aid dated 5 April 2023. Key Issues: Whether the subpoena against the Minister should be issued. MCBA argued the Minister was not a party to the MOA and was not even the Minister when it was executed. Court's Finding: The Court allowed MCBA's objection and refused the subpoena. Applying principles from Wong Sin Chong v Bhagwan Singh and Ismail v Hasnul, the Court held the Minister was not a material witness and the Union was on a fishing expedition amounting to abuse of process. Compensation/Award Details: This was a procedural/interim ruling on a subpoena application. No financial orders or compensation were made. The Court simply refused to issue the Form O (subpoena) against the Minister of Human Resources.

Case 3

Persatuan Bank-Bank Perdagangan Tanah Melayu v National Union of Bank Employees, States of Malaya

No. Kes 22/3-478/24
Award Award No 174 of 2025
Presiding YA Tuan Paramalingam J. Doraisamy
Pages 59-76
Trade Dispute

Summary

Background/Parties: This case (Award No. 174 of 2025) is a trade dispute between MCBA and the National Union of Bank Employees concerning the validity of a Memorandum of Agreement dated 5 April 2023 regarding payment of Festival Aid equivalent to one month's base salary for the year 2023. Key Issues: Whether the MOA has legal or binding effect given it was not deposited for cognizance under Sections 16(1) and 17(2) of the Industrial Relations Act 1967; and whether MCBA is obligated to pay Festival Aid for 2024 onwards. Court's Finding: The Court unanimously ruled the MOA has no legal or binding effect, as it was not deposited with the Registrar. The MOA was a one-off agreement for 2023 only. Compensation/Award Details: No compensation or financial orders were made in favour of the Union. The Court ruled MCBA has no legal obligation to pay Festival Aid post-2023 under the MOA. The one-off payment of Festival Aid equivalent to one month's base salary applied only to 2023.

Outcome

Reinstatement

Compensation

RM500.00

David Chew Yu Lin v BFM Media Sdn Bhd

No. Kes 21(31)(21)/4-726/19
Award Award No 470 of 2024
Presiding YA Tuan Syed Noh Bin Said Nazir @ Syed Nadzir
Pages 87-110
Salah Laku / Misconduct — Sexual Harassment

Summary

Background/Parties: David Chew Yu Lin was employed by BFM Media Sdn Bhd as a Presenter/Producer from 01.01.2010, with a last drawn salary of RM10,100.00 per month plus RM125.00 monthly allowance. He was dismissed on 04.01.2019 following an investigation into allegations of sexual harassment against three female colleagues. Key Issues: Whether the Company proved the charges of sexual harassment on the balance of probabilities; whether delay in reporting was material; and whether the dismissal was proportionate. Court's Finding: The Court found the Company proved the charges. Applying the twofold test for sexual harassment, the Court held the touching was sexual in nature and unwelcome. The Claimant offered only bare denials and evasive testimony, while complainants were credible. Delay in reporting was explained by social stigma. Dismissal was proportionate given the employer's duty to provide a safe working environment. Compensation/Award Details: The claim was dismissed and no compensation was awarded to the Claimant. The Court held the dismissal was with just cause or excuse for proven misconduct of sexual harassment.

Outcome

Dismissal without just cause or excuse Reinstatement

The Union's application for 8% interest on arrears and costs was dismissed.

Dalton Wen Fong Simon Awang v KNM Exotic Equipment Sdn Bhd

No. Kes 1/1-1186/23
Award Award No 787 of 2024
Presiding YA Dato Wan Jeffry bin Kassim
Pages 496-505
Non-Compliance

Summary

Background/Parties: Dalton Wen Fong Anak Simon Awang filed a non-compliance complaint under Section 56(1) against KNM Exotic Equipment Sdn Bhd for failing to pay RM105,000.00 as ordered in Consent Award No. 1092 of 2023, which required payment by 12.06.2023. Key Issues: Three defences: ultra vires representative, financial incapacity, and a Restraining Order granted to the parent company. Court's Finding: All three defences rejected. The Turquand Rule applied -- the Complainant was entitled to assume the Company's Legal Advisor had authority. Financial incapacity is not a special circumstance under Section 56(2)(c). The Restraining Order named only the parent company, not the Respondent. Compensation/Award Details: The Court ordered the Respondent Company to pay RM105,000.00 forthwith as stipulated in the original Consent Award. No special circumstances were found to justify varying or setting aside the Award.
